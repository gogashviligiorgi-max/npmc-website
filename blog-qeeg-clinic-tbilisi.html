What Is QEEG?

QEEG (Quantitative Electroencephalography) records and digitally analyzes the brain's electrical activity. Unlike a standard EEG used to rule out epilepsy, QEEG measures simultaneous activity across 19 brain regions, compares your results to a normative database of healthy individuals, and produces a visual brain map — a color-coded image showing exactly where and how your brain differs from normal function.

The result is a personalized neurological fingerprint — the scientific foundation for everything that follows.

How It Works

1

Preparation (5 min)

A lightweight EEG cap with 19 electrodes is placed on your head. Completely painless — the electrodes only measure, they emit nothing.

2

Recording (20–30 min)

You sit comfortably. Eyes open for 15 minutes, eyes closed for 15 minutes. Brain electrical signals are recorded.

3

Analysis (24–48 hours)

A specialist processes the data and compares it to age-matched normative data.

4

Consultation

You receive a full color brain map, a detailed written report (in English or Georgian), and an individualized treatment protocol.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is QEEG painful or invasive?

No. The electrodes are passive sensors placed on the scalp with a conductive gel. No electrical stimulation, no needles, no discomfort.

Can I get QEEG without committing to treatment?

Yes. The "Brain Check" service is a standalone brain profile — no follow-up treatment required.

Do you offer consultations in English?

Yes. All consultations, reports, and patient communication are available in English.

How do I prepare for QEEG?

Avoid caffeine on the day of recording. Come with clean, dry hair (no styling products). Get a full night's sleep beforehand.
