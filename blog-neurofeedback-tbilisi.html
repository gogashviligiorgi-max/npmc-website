How Neurofeedback Works

1

Sensors measure your brain

Your brain's electrical activity is measured in real time via sensors on your scalp.

2

Software processes the signal

Data is processed within milliseconds.

3

Your brain receives feedback

When your brain produces the target pattern, the screen brightens or a sound plays — a "reward" signal.

4

Your brain learns

Through operant conditioning, the brain learns to generate the target pattern more often. This is neuroplasticity in practical application.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is neurofeedback safe?

Yes — completely. The sensors only measure brain activity; they emit nothing. No electrical stimulation. FDA-recognized as safe. No reported serious adverse effects in decades of clinical use.

How soon will I notice results?

Many patients notice subtle changes (calmer, better focused, improved sleep) within 3–5 sessions. Significant clinical improvement typically by 10–15 sessions.

Do you offer sessions in English?

Yes. All consultations, reports, and communication are available in English.
